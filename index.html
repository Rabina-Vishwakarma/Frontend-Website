First of all, I greet everyone while speaking on the important issue of improving the transportation system in the rural areas of Maharashtra.

An extremely important scheme has been proposed to make transportation in the rural areas of Maharashtra more effective and accessible. Based on the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana, it is essential to create a small bridge (Sakav) plan that connects each village to another. This will make communication between rural areas easier and strengthen the transportation system. Approximately 500 to 600 villages in Maharashtra require the construction of small bridges. The expected cost for building these bridges is around 50 lakh to 5 crore rupees. In this regard, it is necessary for the central government to prepare a comprehensive plan and propose financial assistance for the construction of these bridges. For this purpose, I am presenting it to the Union Minister of Roads, Transport, and Highways, Nitin Gadkari ji, requesting guidance for preparing a proposal in line with all necessary requirements.

In my view, the importance of this is not just for Maharashtra, but for all the rural areas of the country. Because, when transportation improves in rural areas, that becomes the path to development. Furthermore, Maharashtra currently has about 3 lakh kilometers of roads. Due to the efforts of Gadkari ji, many state highways have been converted into national highways, which has reduced some of our responsibilities. However, for the construction of an additional 96,000 kilometers of roads in Maharashtra, approximately 2 lakh crore rupees are required. Raising such a massive amount is extremely challenging for the state budget. Therefore, additional financial assistance from the central government is essential, which will benefit not only Maharashtra but the entire country. Therefore, I am confident that if the central government extends more cooperation to Maharashtra in this regard, there will be significant development not just in that region, but across the entire country.

Thank You!
